What can a webpage do?

Text: As the word "page" suggests, a webpage can display text. Pages can also feature links to other pages. Much of web design consists of displaying text much as a hard copy book would. Web pages can feature text in different sizes, colors, fonts, background color, opacity, and positioning. However, this is not the end of what it can do, but the beginning.

Two-way flow of information: A book allows information to flow in only one direction. In technical parlance, this is labeled "simplex". While books can be wonderful entertainment and tools for learning, a book is only sending information. The book itself cannot receive data. Websites, on the other hand, can collect information from those visiting them. "Form" is the technical name for the HTML structures that illicit information from visitors. These can take various . . . um . . . forms. Some can take a simple query about a visitor's preference to display the page in a particular way. Others might send information to a database administering a newsletter. But, the point is that, unlike a newspaper ad or flyer, the website creates a more interactive relationship with one's intended audience.

Accessibility: While most of your site's visitors will read your page in the form of text, some will not be able to. But, as strange as it may seem, given how largely visual the Internet is, it has proven an incredible resource for the blind. Internet screen readers are utilities that scan web pages and output the text in audio form. Screen readers can also describe images, links, and other media displayed on the page.

Multi-media: Beyond mere text, websites can also display static or moving images, play sound and music, and allow two-way communication between users with a delay or in real-time.

Social Media: As a cultural, economic, and technological outgrowth of the fact that the Internet can disseminate AND collect information, social media have been developed. Applications like Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, and others can powerfully augment the effectiveness of your website. Political organizations, cultural endeavors, businesses, and other entities can find these platforms to be effective at leveraging their efforts.

Search Engine Optimization: Having a memorable website address can be a great way to ensure that people who have heard of your site or visited it before can find it when they look for it. However, the truth is that many people looking for your site may not know that it, or something like it, even exists. They will use search engines, like Google or Bing, to locate it.
